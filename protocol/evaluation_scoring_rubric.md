# Evaluation Scoring Rubric

Score each dimension from **0 to 5**. Evaluate only what is explicitly present in the response — do not infer or assume.

---

## 1. Evidence Quality

**Definition:** How well the response cites credible, verifiable, recent, and relevant sources.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|:---|
| **5** | ≥3 peer-reviewed citations (journals, top conferences); complete details (author + year + venue + DOI); appropriately recent for topic type; all major claims directly supported; evidence strength explicitly labeled (e.g., ESTABLISHED / EMERGING / SPECULATIVE); quantitative metrics provided |
| **4** | 2–3 peer-reviewed citations; most citations include author + year + venue (DOIs may be missing); recency appropriate for most claims; major claims cited with 1–2 minor gaps; some evidence categorization; some quantitative detail |
| **3** | 1–2 peer-reviewed sources OR multiple lower-tier sources; basic citation info (author + year); mix of recent and older sources; some major claims cited, others use generic references ("studies show"); no systematic evidence labeling |
| **2** | 0–1 peer-reviewed sources; heavy reliance on generic references; many claims uncited; no evidence categorization; no quantitative metrics |
| **1** | Zero verifiable citations; all claims unattributed; no way to verify information |
| **0** | Entirely unsupported assertions; no citation attempt of any kind |

---

## 2. Transparency

**Definition:** How honestly the response discloses limitations, boundary conditions, biases, and applicability constraints.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|:---|
| **5** | ≥5 distinct limitation types disclosed (e.g., sample size, population, domain, temporal, methodological); ≥3 explicit boundary conditions (when/where/for whom it does NOT apply); ≥2 bias types acknowledged; ≥2 knowledge gaps identified; >80% of claims contextualized; appropriate hedging throughout; competing views acknowledged |
| **4** | 3–4 limitation types; 2–3 boundary conditions; ≥1 bias acknowledged; some knowledge gaps noted; 60–80% of claims contextualized; frequent hedging language |
| **3** | 1–2 limitation types; 1–2 boundary conditions; limited bias acknowledgment; few knowledge gaps; occasional hedging; rarely acknowledges alternatives |
| **2** | Vague limitation mention only (e.g., "more research needed"); no explicit boundaries; no bias acknowledgment; mostly definitive statements |
| **1** | No limitations acknowledged; no qualifications; overconfident throughout |
| **0** | Actively presents findings as universal; no transparency of any kind |

---

## 3. Reasoning Depth

**Definition:** How well the response explains WHY and HOW, not just WHAT — showing mechanisms, causal chains, and theoretical grounding.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|:---|
| **5** | >60% of explanations are multi-step or theoretically grounded (A→B→C or theory-linked); all major claims include mechanism explanations; ≥2 theories explicitly integrated and applied; logically coherent throughout with no fallacies; >80% of causal chains complete; concrete examples illustrate abstract concepts |
| **4** | 40–60% of explanations at multi-step/theoretical level; most major claims have mechanisms; ≥2 theories referenced and applied; generally coherent logic; most causal chains complete (60–80%); some examples provided |
| **3** | 20–40% of explanations at multi-step/theoretical level; some mechanisms present, others missing; 1–2 theories mentioned with limited integration; logical but basic reasoning; some causal chains incomplete |
| **2** | Mostly surface-level explanations (correlations stated, no mechanism); few mechanism explanations; theory rarely mentioned; incomplete causal chains |
| **1** | Mostly assertions without explanation; no mechanism or theoretical grounding; no causal reasoning |
| **0** | Pure assertion; no explanation of any kind |

---

## 4. Actionability

**Definition:** How well the response provides specific, practical, implementable recommendations with clear guidance on execution.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|:---|
| **5** | ≥3 specific recommendations (quantified or precisely described); implementation steps provided (HOW to execute); clear contextual applicability (for whom, when, under what conditions); success criteria or metrics specified; resource requirements disclosed; recommendations prioritized or sequenced; practical examples or templates included |
| **4** | 2–3 specific recommendations; some implementation guidance; context mostly clear; some success criteria; some resource info; implicit or partial prioritization; some examples |
| **3** | 1–2 specific OR several moderate recommendations; minimal implementation guidance; some context; vague success criteria; no clear prioritization |
| **2** | Vague recommendations (abstract, no detail); little or no implementation guidance; context minimal or absent; no success criteria |
| **1** | Very vague suggestions; no guidance on how to act; cannot be implemented from information provided |
| **0** | No recommendations of any kind |

---

## 5. Uncertainty Disclosure

**Definition:** How well the response communicates the confidence level of its claims — labeling uncertain findings, flagging contested areas, and acknowledging gaps in knowledge.

| Score | Criteria |
|:---:|:---|
| **5** | All claims appropriately labeled by confidence level (e.g., ESTABLISHED / EMERGING / SPECULATIVE or equivalent); uncertain/preliminary findings explicitly marked; contested areas acknowledged with competing evidence; knowledge gaps clearly identified; no unverifiable claims stated as definitive fact; calibrated language throughout |
| **4** | Most claims carry appropriate confidence labels or hedging; 1–2 unverifiable claims present but qualified (e.g., "may", "preliminary"); knowledge gaps noted; minor overstatement at most |
| **3** | Some confidence labeling or hedging present; a few unverifiable claims stated as fact without qualification; some knowledge gaps mentioned; inconsistent across claims |
| **2** | Minimal hedging; several unverifiable claims presented as definitive fact; knowledge gaps largely absent; mostly overconfident tone |
| **1** | No uncertainty disclosure; multiple unverifiable or contested claims stated as certain fact |
| **0** | Entirely overconfident; no acknowledgment of uncertainty or limitations on any claim |

---

## Scoring Notes

- **Score each dimension independently.** A response can score 5 on Actionability and 1 on Evidence Quality.
- **Base scores only on what is explicitly stated.** Do not reward implied or assumed content.
- **For responses with no citations:** Evidence Quality ≤ 2; Uncertainty Disclosure ≤ 3 unless explicit confidence labels are used without citations.
- **Total score** = sum of all five dimensions (max 25).
